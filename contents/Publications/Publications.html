  • SPIE Optics+Photonics, San Diego, CA, USA, August 1st-5th, 2011


A Monte Carlo approach for simulating the propagation of partially coherent x-ray beams.

A, Prodi et.al.

Advances at SR sources in the generation of nanofocused beams with a high degree of transverse coherence call for effective techniques to simulate the propagation of partially coherent X-ray beams through complex optical systems in order to characterize how coherence properties such as the mutual coherence function (MCF) are propagated to the exit plane. Here we present an approach based on Monte Carlo sampling of the Green function.


McXtrace: a modern ray-tracing package for x-ray instrumentation

E. Knudsen et.al.

Here we present the developments of the McXtrace project, a free, open source software package based on Monte Carlo ray tracing for simulations and optimization of complete X-ray instruments.


Advanced simulations of x-ray beam propagation through CRL "transfocators" using ray-tracing and wavefront propagation methods

J. Baltser et.al.

At present, Compound Refractive Lenses (CRL) are widely used to manipulate X-ray range synchrotron radiation beams. Accurate modelling of X-ray beam propagation through individual CRLs and through "transfocators" composed out of large number of CRLs is of high importance, since it allows for comprehensive optimization of X-ray beamline designs for particular user experiments.
